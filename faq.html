Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about staying at Hale Moku Inn.

Standard check-in is 3pm and check-out is 11am. Early check-in (from 1pm) and late check-out (until 1pm) may be available depending on occupancy — just let us know and we'll do our best to accommodate. We're always happy to store luggage if your room isn't ready when you arrive.
Yes! Complimentary breakfast is served every morning from 7am–9:30am. We serve fresh seasonal local fruit, baked pastries, island eggs prepared to order, fresh-squeezed juice, and Kona coffee or tea. Our chef Lana has been with us since day one and her pineapple upside-down pancakes are famous among returning guests. Just let us know any dietary restrictions when you book.
The most popular way is the Expeditions ferry from Lahaina Harbor, which takes about 45 minutes and costs approximately $30 per person each way. Book online at golanai.com. There are also commuter flights from Honolulu and Maui via Ohana by Hawaiian and Mokulele Airlines. We recommend the ferry for the scenic experience — you may spot dolphins or humpback whales on the crossing.
Yes! We offer transfers from Lanai Airport and Manele Harbor (Expeditions ferry dock) to the inn for $25/person. Just include your arrival details when you book and we'll coordinate pickup. This is highly recommended as taxis and rideshares on Lanai are limited.
We welcome well-behaved dogs in our Garden Rooms and Plumeria Cottage with prior approval. There is a $35/night pet fee. Dogs must be kept on leash in the garden areas and are not permitted in the dining room during breakfast. Please let us know when booking if you're bringing a pet.
Yes, complimentary Wi-Fi is available throughout the inn. Fair warning: internet speeds on Lanai are slower than what you may be used to — we're a remote island, after all. For most browsing, streaming, and video calls it's adequate, but if you need high-speed internet for intensive work, please contact us to discuss options.
We can arrange: snorkeling at Manele Bay, 4WD jeep rentals for exploring the island, guided hiking tours, whale watching cruises (December–April), dolphin watching tours, kayak rentals, and dinner reservations at the Four Seasons resort restaurants. We know Lanai inside and out and love helping guests have the perfect island experience.
During peak season (June–August, December–January), we have a 2-night minimum stay. During the rest of the year, single nights are available. We generally recommend at least 2 nights on Lanai to truly experience the island — one day is simply not enough!
Cancellations made 14 or more days before check-in receive a full refund. Cancellations 7–13 days before check-in receive a 50% refund. Cancellations less than 7 days before check-in are non-refundable. We recommend travel insurance, particularly given Lanai's ferry-dependent access which can occasionally be affected by weather.
