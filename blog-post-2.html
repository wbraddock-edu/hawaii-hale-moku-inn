Lanai vs Maui — Which Island to Visit

Island Comparison · May 28, 2025 · 5 min read

We hear this question constantly from guests who are planning their Hawaii trip and trying to decide whether to add Lanai to their itinerary or spend their entire time on Maui. Having lived on Lanai for 18 years and knowing Maui well, here's our honest perspective.

The Case for Maui

Maui has more of everything: more beaches (76 at last count), more restaurants, more activities, more accommodation options at every price point, and more infrastructure for travelers. The Road to Hana is one of the most famous drives in the world for good reason. Haleakala's volcanic crater is a truly otherworldly experience. Lahaina and Kaanapali offer the full resort Hawaii experience. If this is your only Hawaii trip, Maui delivers the most diverse range of experiences.

The Case for Lanai

Lanai offers something increasingly rare in Hawaii: genuine quiet. With only about 3,200 residents and limited tourism development, you can stand on Polihua Beach — one of the most beautiful beaches in Hawaii — and have it completely to yourself. You can drive the dirt roads through the upcountry on a rented jeep and feel like you're on a safari. The dolphins at Manele Bay are so accustomed to gentle visitors that they sometimes swim beside snorkelers.

The Practical Answer

Do both if you can. Lanai makes a perfect 2–3 night addition to a longer Maui trip — the ferry takes less than an hour and runs twice daily. Think of Maui as your dynamic, diverse base and Lanai as your peaceful retreat. Many visitors tell us that the two nights on Lanai were the highlight of their entire trip.

Who Lanai Is Perfect For

Lanai is ideal for: couples seeking romance and quiet (honeymoons are popular here), solo travelers who want authentic Hawaii without crowds, families who want space and a slower pace, and repeat Hawaii visitors who have done the main islands and are ready for something different.
