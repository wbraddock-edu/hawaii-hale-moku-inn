Traveling to Lanai from Maui Ferry

Getting Here · Apr 5, 2025 · 5 min read

The most popular way to reach Lanai from Maui is via the Expeditions ferry, which runs between Lahaina Harbor and Manele Harbor (Lanai). It's a wonderfully scenic crossing that takes about 45 minutes to an hour depending on sea conditions, and it's a genuinely fun part of the Lanai experience. Here's everything you need to know.

Booking the Ferry

Expeditions Ferry runs multiple daily crossings — typically four or five in each direction, with the first departure from Lahaina around 6:30am. We strongly recommend booking online at the Expeditions website in advance, especially during peak season (June–August, December–January). The ferry is popular and often sells out, particularly on weekends. Current pricing is around $30 per person each way.

What to Expect

The Kapalua, the main vessel, holds about 150 passengers. In summer, the channel crossing is generally calm and beautiful — you may see humpback whales between December and April. In winter and during periods of high south swell, the channel can be rough. If you're prone to seasickness, take Dramamine or wear Sea-Bands before boarding.

Arriving at Manele Harbor

Manele Harbor is on Lanai's southern coast, about 9 miles from Lanai City. There is no bus service connecting them, so you'll need to either rent a vehicle, arrange a ride, or take a taxi. We offer a ferry pickup service for our guests for $25/person — just let us know your arrival time when you book and we'll be there waiting.

Getting Around Lanai

Once on Lanai, transportation options are limited. There are a small number of rental cars and 4WD jeeps available from Dollar Rent A Car and a few local operators — book these well in advance as the island has only a handful of vehicles available. For getting around Lanai City itself, everything is walkable. For beach access and exploration, a 4WD vehicle is strongly recommended.
