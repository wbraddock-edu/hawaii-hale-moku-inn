Hidden Gems of Lanai Island

Local Secrets · Mar 18, 2025 · 5 min read

After 18 years of living and hosting on Lanai, we've accumulated a list of places and experiences that don't appear in most travel guides. These are the spots we share with guests who ask for the "real Lanai" — the side of the island that takes a little effort to find and rewards you exponentially for the search.

The Munro Trail at Dawn

The Munro Trail is a 12-mile ridge trail that traverses the spine of Lanaihale, Lanai's highest peak at 3,366 feet. On a clear morning — which requires catching a narrow weather window — you can see all eight of the main Hawaiian islands from the summit. We check the forecast for guests and wake them if conditions are right. The drive to the trailhead in a 4WD vehicle through the misty pine forest is itself magical.

Garden of the Gods (Keahiakawelo)

This geological marvel of wind-sculpted rock formations on Lanai's northwestern plateau looks like something from another planet. Ancient Hawaiian legend says the gods engaged in a fire-making contest here. The rocks, in shades of red, orange, and purple, are particularly dramatic at sunset and sunrise. It's accessible by dirt road and free to visit.

Kaunolu Village Site

This ancient Hawaiian fishing village on the southern cliffs is one of Hawaii's most significant archaeological sites — and one of its least visited. Kamehameha the Great's summer house was here. The dramatic clifftop location, the preserved heiau, and the 90-foot sea cliffs make this a profoundly moving place. Only accessible by 4WD and a short hike. We arrange guided visits for guests who want historical context.

Nighttime Stargazing

Lanai has virtually no light pollution. On a clear night at the Garden of the Gods or the Munro Trail, the Milky Way is so vivid it looks painted. We lend guests our red-light flashlights and binoculars for nighttime explorations. The Big Island's Mauna Kea gets all the astronomy attention, but Lanai's stargazing is extraordinary and accessible without any altitude discomfort.

The Plantation Manager's House

A preserved colonial-era home in Lanai City that has been converted into a small museum and community space. The building itself is a remarkable piece of social history — the physical architecture of plantation hierarchy is visible in its design. Many visitors walk past it without realizing what they're seeing. Ask us for the full story.
