Best Restaurants in Lanai City

Food Guide · Apr 22, 2025 · 5 min read

Lanai City's dining scene is small but genuinely excellent — a combination of plantation-era plate lunch joints, local family restaurants, and a few surprisingly sophisticated spots. Here are our honest recommendations as innkeepers who eat here every day.

Café 565

This is our absolute go-to for lunch. Café 565 (named for the island's telephone exchange prefix) serves simple, beautifully executed sandwiches, soups, and plate lunches. The garlic shrimp plate is legendary on the island. It's located in a colorful plantation home and has a small garden patio. Arrive early — they sell out.

Pele's Other Garden

The best dinner spot in Lanai City by a significant margin. This wood-paneled restaurant serves excellent pizza, pasta, and deli sandwiches in a warm, casual atmosphere. The wine list is thoughtful and well-priced. On weekend evenings, it fills with a mix of locals and visitors — one of the few places where those two groups genuinely mingle.

The Local Gentry

More of a boutique-café than a full restaurant, but worth mentioning. Excellent coffee, fresh pastries, and a curated selection of local products. A great spot to start your morning before heading out to explore the island. The owner, Jennifer Ahern-Lipe, has been a pillar of the Lanai City community for years.

Our Breakfast

We may be biased, but Hale Moku Inn's complimentary breakfast is genuinely one of the best meals guests have on the island. We serve fresh local fruits (seasonal), baked goods, island eggs, and Kona coffee each morning. Our breakfast chef, Lana Reyes, has been cooking for the inn since we opened in 2008 — her pineapple upside-down pancakes are famous among returning guests.
